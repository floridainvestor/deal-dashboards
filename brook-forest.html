Risk Flags + Decision

Cross-Strategy Risk · Verdict · Next Steps
⚠ Smaller scale (4 rooms vs. 7–9) limits upside
1,100 sqft on 3 bedrooms limits this to 4 rooms at base. Co-living returns are scale-dependent: Skycrest at 9 rooms drives 45% CoC; 4 rooms here drives only 10.9%. The 5th room is not optional if you need 20%+ CoC. Verify floor plan physically supports 5 legal rooms before committing.
4-Room PadSplit below 20% CoC target
At $24.5K all-in with 4 rooms ($2,730/mo gross), CoC lands at 10.9% — 9 points short of the 20% floor. This is not a rounding error. Do not close on the assumption that 4 rooms will suffice. Either renegotiate entry below $8K or commit to the 5th room before signing.
Sub-To due-on-sale clause (DOSC) exposure
Inheriting this loan triggers DOSC. The 5.87% rate is not exceptional — lender has moderate incentive to call the loan. Mitigate with land trust vesting and a sub-to-experienced title company. Never use the seller's title company without confirming they understand the structure. Monitor servicer communications post-close.
May vacancy gap — HUD tenant departing
HUD tenant leaves May 2026. From vacancy to first PadSplit tenant, you carry $1,348/mo PITI with zero revenue. Get furnishings ordered, rooms listed, and Dre engaged before or immediately at close. Every week of vacancy on 5 rooms = ~$770 lost revenue. Target Day 1 occupancy.
32208 C-class neighborhood demand risk
NW Jacksonville — higher vacancy rates, tenant quality risk, and maintenance profile vs. 32246 Skycrest. PadSplit demand in 32208 needs explicit confirmation. Run PadSplit comps in this specific zip before close. The $150–$180/wk comp range must be verified, not assumed.
HOA and zoning status unknown
Any HOA prohibiting rooming houses or co-living kills the entire PadSplit strategy. Zoning in 32208 must also be confirmed for multi-occupant use. This is a binary kill switch — confirm before spending more time on this deal. Run county records check immediately.
Lowest all-in of any sub-to deal — $24.5K
$24,500 gross entry is the most capital-efficient acquisition in the portfolio. Even if 5-room conversion adds $3K, total all-in of $27.5K still produces 40%+ CoC. This gives maximum margin of safety and fastest payback. Small check, large upside — if execution delivers.
Fully amortizing loan — building equity every month
Unlike interest-only seller finance deals, the inherited 5.87% loan pays down principal monthly. Year 1 paydown: $3,038. This is free equity accumulation that compounds over 23 years. At Year 5, balance drops to ~$126K vs. $143K at close — $17K equity gain on top of cash flow returns.
PITI is estimated — taxes and insurance unconfirmed
PITI of $1,348/mo includes estimated taxes ($250) and insurance ($150). If Duval PAO shows higher assessed value or insurance quote comes back elevated for co-living use, every number in this analysis shifts. Pull actual tax bill and get a landlord co-living policy quote before signing.
Seller's title company — sub-to compatibility
Seller insists on their title company. Not all title companies execute sub-to/land trust structures correctly. Confirm the title company has experience before proceeding. If they can't handle land trust vesting, negotiate to use your own title company or walk on this point.
HUD income bridge — confirmed through May
$1,500/mo HUD guaranteed until end of May lease. Cash flows from day 1 of ownership. If you close in April, 4–6 weeks of HUD income to set up PadSplit transition. The HUD payment exceeds PITI, so every week of HUD coverage = positive net cash flow while you prepare.
Servicer status unknown — must confirm
Servicer and loan status are listed as unknown. Any escrow deficiency, forbearance, or missed payment history becomes your problem at close. Call the servicer before the inspection period expires. Verify: current balance, payment history, escrow status, no forbearance, no default.
