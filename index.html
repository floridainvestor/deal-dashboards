Risk Flags · Negotiation · Decision

Due Diligence · Leverage · Next Steps
Title — Junior Liens
Run full title search before committing $29K. Sub-to deals inherit the property "as-is." Any junior lien (IRS, HOA, mechanic's) could surface at payoff. This is a hard stop — walk if liens exist beyond the first mortgage.
Conversion Feasibility — Must Walk First
7-room conversion from 4BD/2.5BA is the core assumption. 2,252 SF supports it mathematically but the floor plan layout may not. Walk the property and get a contractor quote before the entry fee is non-refundable.
Mortgage Servicer — Sub-To Compatibility
Confirm the servicer does not have an active "due-on-sale" enforcement posture. Some servicers flag title transfers. Get servicer name from wholesaler before close and verify payment history.
2014 AC Condenser — Near End of Life
Average condenser lifespan is 15–20 years. This one is 12 years old. Budget $3,000–$5,000 for replacement within 3 years. Negotiate a credit from the wholesaler or include in conversion budget. The air handler (2022) is fine.
PadSplit Market Comp — 32218 Rate Validation
$700/room is the wholesaler's estimate. Verify on the PadSplit host site for 32218 (North Jax / near Airport). If comps support $650–$700, base case holds. If market is below $625, re-run numbers before buying.
Rate Lock — Significant Advantage
5.25% sub-to vs. 7.5%+ DSCR market rate saves approximately $600/mo in debt service over a new loan. This rate lock is the structural advantage that makes the deal work. Protect it.
No HOA — PadSplit Friendly
HOAs frequently restrict or ban co-living / room rental arrangements. No HOA removes this regulatory risk entirely. City of Jacksonville permits PadSplit operations in residential zones — no special use permit required.
Roof + Plumbing — Clean Mechanicals
2018 roof (8 years old), PVC plumbing, city water/sewer. No polybutylene or copper risk. No septic. These are the items that kill margins on PadSplit conversions — this property avoids all of them.

Negotiation Leverage — Ask For Before Signing

1
AC Condenser Credit — $3,000–$5,000
"The condenser is 12 years old — it's going to need replacement within a few years. I need a $4,000 credit at closing or a reduction in the entry fee to cover it."
Wholesaler is making $18,803 on assignment. They have room. This drops your all-in to ~$58K and bumps CoC to 18.8%.
2
30-Day Inspection Period Before Entry Fee Goes Hard
"I need 30 days to walk the property, get a contractor conversion quote, and clear title before my entry fee becomes non-refundable."
Standard on any subject-to deal. If the wholesaler won't give you an inspection period, that's a red flag about the condition or title.
3
Confirm CC + TC Fee Cap (~$2,500 max)
"What exactly is included in CC and TC fees? I need those itemized and capped in the purchase agreement."
Vague "CC + TC" language can balloon. Get it in writing before you sign anything.
4
Confirm Assignment Fee Is Final — No Hidden Fees
"Just confirming the $29K entry is all-in for your side. No additional fees, no wholesaler split at closing?"
Some wholesalers stack fees at the title company. Confirm the net to you in writing.
