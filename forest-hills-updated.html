Break-even math: Total monthly nut (fixed expenses + debt service) = $2,917 + $1,583 = $4,500/mo. Each room at $165/wk = $715/mo. $4,500 ÷ $715 = 6.29 rooms. So 7 of 9 rooms occupied covers every fixed cost + debt. That leaves 2 rooms of cash flow margin. At 91% occupancy (8.19 rooms paying), the cushion is 1.90 rooms above break-even. Note: this deal does NOT pay back the $111,400 all-in within the 10-year note term under base-case assumptions.

Forest Hills vs. Skycrest — The Honest Read

With all 9 rooms shared at $165/wk, 91% occupancy, and a $100K midpoint rehab budget, the numbers work — but this deal is fundamentally tighter than Skycrest.

Monthly cash flow: $594/mo  ·  Annual: $7,128  ·  CoC: 6.4%  ·  DSCR: 1.38×  ·  Cap Rate: 6.9%

Skycrest generates 45% CoC on $52K. Forest Hills generates 6.4% CoC on $111K. The gap is almost entirely explained by the rehab budget: $100K for a 1950 build with an indoor pool vs. $40K for a newer structure. If the rehab comes in at $80K, CoC improves to ~8.3%. At the worst-case $150K, CoC drops to ~4.7%.

The critical variables in order of impact:

1. Rehab budget — get a firm GC bid with pool scoped separately before committing. 2. Electric bill — verify actual JEA history; 1950 construction + 5,775 sqft runs $1,000/mo based on updated estimates. 3. Rate structure — this deal was designed with a potential step-up rate structure (built separately). At 5% flat IO for 10 years, the 10-year balloon removes near-term refi pressure, but rates must stay serviceable. 4. Room rates — $165/wk is below Skycrest ($200/wk) and below the PadSplit market for this zip. JAX 32208 may support $175-185/wk; each $5/wk increase adds ~$195/mo to NOI.

Verdict: This deal clears all basic hurdles — positive cash flow, DSCR above 1.25×, 70% break-even, $0 down. But the 188-month payback means you don't get your $111K back within the 10-year note term. The indoor pool is both the most compelling amenity (marketing) and the highest risk item (maintenance, insurance, liability). This deal is a YES at $80K rehab and a careful YES at $100K. At $120K+, renegotiate the price down to $350K or pass.
