Risk Flags + Decision

Cross-Strategy Risk · Sub-To Specific · Verdict · Next Steps
Due-on-Sale Clause — Sub-To Risk
All conventional mortgages contain a due-on-sale clause. Taking title Subject-To triggers technical default if the lender discovers the transfer. Mitigation: acquire via land trust + performance deed, keep payments current, avoid triggering lender notice (insurance changes, tax bill address, etc.). Confirm servicer's enforcement posture before closing — some servicers are more aggressive than others. This is the primary systemic risk of the entire deal structure.
Conversion Feasibility — Must Walk First
7-room conversion from 4BD/2.5BA is the core income assumption. 2,252 SF supports it on paper (~322 SF/room average) but the actual floor plan layout must be confirmed. Walk the property, get a contractor quote, and confirm PadSplit room standards (egress windows, locking doors, minimum size) before the entry fee goes non-refundable. If only 6 rooms are achievable, re-run numbers.
Title Search — Junior Liens
Sub-To deals inherit the property as-is. Run a full title search before committing $29K. Look for: judgment liens, IRS liens, code enforcement liens, mechanic's liens, undisclosed second mortgages. Any junior lien beyond the first mortgage is a hard stop — renegotiate or walk. This step costs ~$200 and protects your entire entry fee.
AC Condenser Age (2014 — 12 Years)
Air handler replaced in 2022 (good). Condenser is 2014 — 12 years old, approaching end of typical 15-year lifespan. Budget $3,000–5,000 for replacement within 2–3 years. Already buffered in CapEx reserve line. Negotiate a $3,000 credit from the wholesaler before signing — they have room in the $18,803 assignment fee. Inspect condenser condition at walk.
PadSplit Comp Verification — 32218
$175/wk is the base case rate assumption for 32218 North Jax. Verify on PadSplit host portal before closing. If market comps support $160–$175, base case holds. If 32218 is clearing below $150/wk, the conservative scenario tightens significantly. Check 3–5 active PadSplit listings in 32218 before submitting offer.
Arrears + Servicer Reinstatement
~$10,197 in arrears must be cured at closing. Get a reinstatement quote from the lender in writing — confirm they accept reinstatement vs. a full payoff demand. Confirm payment history: no missed payments beyond the listed arrears, no force-placed insurance, no escrow shortfall. Some lenders use reinstatement as a tripwire to accelerate the note. Know their stance before funds move.
Rate Lock at 5.25% — Structural Advantage
5.25% Sub-To vs. 7.5%+ current DSCR market rate saves approximately $600/mo in debt service. Over 5 years that's $36,000 in savings vs. market rate financing. This rate lock is the structural reason the deal cash-flows when SFR rental doesn't. Protect it: make payments on time, maintain land trust structure, don't trigger lender notice.
Clean Mechanicals — No HOA, City Utilities, PVC
2018 roof (8 yrs old, ~17 yrs remaining), PVC plumbing (no polybutylene risk), city water/sewer (no septic exposure), no HOA (PadSplit co-living unrestricted). These are the three items that destroy margins on PadSplit conversions. This property avoids all of them. The only mechanical watch is the 2014 condenser.
