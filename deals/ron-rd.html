Risk Flags & Decision

Sub-To Specific Risks · Downside Analysis · Verdict · Next Steps
Risk Flags — 6 Key Items
Sub-To Due-on-Sale Clause
Taking title Subject-To triggers the underlying lender’s due-on-sale clause. Most servicers do not actively enforce if payments continue without interruption, but the risk is real. Any retitling, wrap, or refinance action can surface this. Use a land trust to insulate. Never miss a payment. Do not notify the servicer of ownership change.
Mortgage Rate — Unknown & Critical
PITI of $1,900/mo on ~$150K balance implies P&I of ~$1,393/mo. If rate is 8%: P&I = $1,101 (plausible, leaves room for escrow). If rate is 12%+: P&I = $1,543, escrow is minimal, and this is a predatory-rate loan. Verify rate, term, servicer name, and current/past-due status. This is the go/no-go gate. Do not close without this information.
Tight Room-to-SF Ratio
1,382 sqft for 7 rooms = 197 sqft/room average. Very tight for PadSplit livability. 5 rooms is a more realistic ceiling without garage conversion. If only 5 rooms are approved: CF drops from $697 to −$328/mo — deal becomes a NO. Walk required with tape measure before committing to conversion costs.
Existing Lender Escrow & Insurance Risk
Sub-To deals require maintaining the lender’s existing insurance policy AND obtaining your own landlord/co-living policy. If the original homeowner’s policy lapses or the insurer discovers a non-owner-occupied co-living use, they may cancel or deny a claim. Obtain a non-owner-occupied landlord policy that aligns with co-living use before tenants move in.
Seller $74K Underwater — Leverage + Caution
DSG GROUP LLC paid $229,900 in March 2025. At $156K counter, seller is taking a $74K loss. Leverage for Jay: seller’s alternatives are short sale or foreclosure, both worse outcomes for the LLC. However, a distressed seller can create title complications — verify no judgments, liens, or code violations before close. Pull official records on the property.
Fully Renovated — No Rehab Risk
Seller states brand new everything. If true, eliminates the single largest Sub-To risk: inheriting deferred maintenance. Only cost is the 4-room conversion ($15K) and furnishing. Year 1 maintenance should be minimal. Verify renovation quality at walk — check roof, HVAC, plumbing, and electrical panel age.
