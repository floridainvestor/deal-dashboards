!PropStream Owner/Parcel Match Was Wrong — Loan Now Confirmed Directly

PropStream had named the owner as "Cuttyhunk LLC" off a $257,017 sale dated 5/2/2024, with a mismatched loan of $175,672 @ 6.87%. None of that is this property's chain of title. Duval PAO's official Sales History for RE# 063374-0005 shows no such deed at any point — the only 2023-2024 instrument recorded is the $275,000 Warranty Deed dated 11/27/2023 to Michelle De La Pena, the same owner and same closing date as the other two properties in this pipeline.

The actual recorded Mortgage/Security Instrument (Doc #2023248626, OR Bk 20887 Pg 1276, recorded 12/5/2023, lender Castle Mortgage Corporation DBA Excelerate Capital, Loan #C101017310) confirms: original principal $137,500, Note dated 11/27/2023, 30-year term (maturity 12/1/2053), borrower Michelle De La Pena. That's the real loan on this property — PropStream's figures are disregarded entirely, not just the owner match.

!Flag: 2025 Property Tax Bill Appears Unpaid

Duval County Tax Collector records for 869 Line St and 8032 Moncrief Dinsmore Rd both show "paid in full," most recent payment 11/24/2025. This property's 2025 Annual Bill of $5,127.67 shows no such payment confirmation — it displays as an outstanding "Total amount due," not a paid receipt.

This is consistent with — and likely explains — PropStream's "High" Foreclosure Factor flag on this property (the other two carry no such flag or a lower tier). Combined with the thinnest equity cushion of the three properties, this raises the risk profile meaningfully. If it stays delinquent, Duval County can eventually sell a tax certificate against it — a separate path to losing the property, independent of the mortgage.

Confirm directly with Shell (or a fresh Tax Collector pull) whether this has since been paid before proceeding further.
