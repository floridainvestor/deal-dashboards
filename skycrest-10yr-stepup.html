Pete's Read on This Structure

The step-up from 5% to 8% over 10 years costs you $48,000 in additional interest versus flat 5%. But it buys you something no bank can offer: 10 years of IO paper on a $400K note with no down payment, no appraisal, no underwriting, and no refi risk.

Your worst month in 10 years is $2,965 net cash flow. Your best is $3,427. The spread between them is $462/mo — half a room's rent. The 3% annual rent growth on 9 rooms compounds at ~$2,500/yr in new revenue. The rate step-ups cost $4,000–$8,000/yr in added debt service. By Year 9, the rent growth has overtaken the rate increases and cash flow is climbing again.

Raj's pitch in one sentence: "You get $648,000 over 10 years on a $400K note — $248K in interest alone — with zero management, zero tenants, and a check that shows up every month. The rate goes up every couple years, so you're protected against inflation. And I'll have 10 years of on-time payments to show for it when the balloon comes due."

You collect $366K on $52K invested. Raj collects $648K on a $400K note. Both of you win. That's the Benefit House.
