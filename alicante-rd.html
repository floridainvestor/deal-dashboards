Sub-To Due-On-Sale Risk — Read Before Using This Exit
Sub-To acquisitions carry an underlying mortgage with a due-on-sale clause. Wrapping the property (selling to an end buyer via seller finance while the underlying Sub-To mortgage remains) triggers a potential acceleration event if the lender discovers the transfer. The 4.625% note holder has contractual right to call the loan due upon sale. Mitigation strategies: land trust with beneficial interest transfer (does not trigger DOS in most states), LLC transfer, or simply not wrapping this property. Consult a real estate attorney before executing a wrap on a Sub-To acquisition.
