Ken McElroy's Playbook

The principles that separate profitable operators from struggling landlords

📖 From The ABC's of Real Estate Investing and The Advanced Guide to Real Estate Investing — applied to HGRT's room rental model
🏃 Rule #1: Speed Fills Rooms
"The landlord who answers first, wins the tenant. Every hour of delay is a room your competitor fills."

The McElroy standard is <5 minutes for first contact on any inquiry. Most room rental operators take 4–48 hours. Set up automated GHL response to beat every competitor in your market before they wake up.

💰 Impact: +3 rooms filled/month = +$1,500–$1,800/mo
⚡ Rule #2: 72-Hour Vacancy Turn
"A vacant unit is not resting — it is hemorrhaging cash. Every day empty is a day you can never recover."

The moment a tenant gives notice or leaves, the clock starts. Clean → repair → photograph → list within 72 hours. Caxton A, B, C right now have been empty too long. Felix should be scheduled TODAY. List tomorrow.

💰 Impact: 3 Caxton rooms filled = +$375–$450/wk immediately
💵 Rule #3: Collect Without Apology
"Rent collection is not personal. It is the mechanism that keeps the business alive for everyone — including the tenants."

Every time you waive a late fee or accept a partial without a plan, you train tenants to push boundaries further. Sunday is collection day — period. Mon–Wed grace is already generous. Thu, the fee applies automatically with no discussion needed.

💰 Impact: Consistent fees recover $200–$400/mo in lost revenue
📋 Rule #4: Systems Beat People
"Your property manager should run the checklist, not invent it every week. Document once, repeat forever."

Every process — collection, cleaning schedule, late fee enforcement, inquiry response — needs a written checklist. Andrea should never need to remember what to do. The system tells her. This dashboard IS the system. Print it every Sunday morning.

💰 Impact: Reduces 4–6 hrs/week of management overhead
📊 Rule #5: Measure RevPAR Weekly
"Revenue per available room is the only number that tells you the truth about your portfolio's health."

RevPAR = (Total Revenue Collected) ÷ (Total Rooms Available). HGRT current: ~$115/room/wk. Target: $135+. The gap is vacancy drag and underpayment. Fix those two things and RevPAR moves automatically.

💰 Impact: Each $10 RevPAR gain = +$820/wk across 82 rooms
🔧 Rule #6: Maintenance = Retention
"Tenants who feel cared for stay longer. A $50 repair today prevents a $500 turnover cost next month."

Respond to every maintenance request within 24 hours — even if it's just to say "Felix will be there Thursday." Silence breeds resentment. Resentment causes move-outs. Move-outs cost you 3–4 weeks of rent. Log every issue, track every cost, send Felix with a clear work order.

💰 Impact: Retain 2 more tenants/year = +$13,000+ in avoided turnover
